The schools were residential institutions operated by the province to house children between the ages of 8 and 16 who were deemed by the courts to be “incorrigible” or difficult to control. A Star investigation found that the province secretly settled 220 lawsuits launched by individual former training school residents over allegations of sexual, physical and psychological abuse at the schools.
