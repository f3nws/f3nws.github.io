Baauer's "Harlem Shake" may have obliterated the internet in 2012, but now the producer is taking on the FCC for using it in a (very dumb) net neutrality repeal video without permission. SEE ALSO: Net neutrality is officially dead The creator of the viral track, which became a 2013 meme thanks to thousands of dancing challenge YouTube videos , has vowed revenge on the FCC and its chairman Ajit Pai. "Harlem Shake" was used by Pai and conservative outlet The Daily Caller in a video entitled "7 Things You Can Still Do on the Internet After Net Neutrality," released after the Federal Communications Commission voted on Thursday to repeal protections for net neutrality .
