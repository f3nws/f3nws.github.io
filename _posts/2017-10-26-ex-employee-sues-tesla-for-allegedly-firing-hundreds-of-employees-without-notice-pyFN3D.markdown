A recently terminated Tesla employee claims the company broke California law when it let hundreds of workers go in what it claims were performance-related firings. In a lawsuit filed Tuesday, Abraham Duarte alleges that employees were not given the proper forewarning required by state law, Jalopnik first reported. California's WARN act compels businesses to provide at least 60 days notice before laying off more than 50 workers.
