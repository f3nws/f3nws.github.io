Uber noted in a statement yesterday that two hackers gained access to personal data on 57 million of its users worldwide and some 600,000 drivers in the US, which was stored on a third-party cloud service. The $70 billion company kept this a secret for about a year. The trove of customer data included names, email addresses and mobile phone numbers; Uber said that its forensic investigation didn’t show that other information like credit card numbers or location history was stolen.
