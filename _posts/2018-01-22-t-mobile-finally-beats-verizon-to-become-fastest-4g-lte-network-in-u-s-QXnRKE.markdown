T-Mobile has been crowned the top wireless network in the U.S., according to OpenSignal's "2018 State of Mobile Networks " report. The "uncarrier" blew past Verizon, AT&T, and Sprint in five out of six major metric tests, besting the competition on 4G download speed, 3G download speed, overall download speed, 3G latency, and 4G availability. The only metric T-Mobile didn't win was 4G latency; AT&T just beat it by a hair. T-Mobile was locked in a tie with Verizon for top mobile carrier this time last year, but now it's taken the lead. And it was all thanks to the kneecapping Verizon and AT&T gave themselves after re-introducing unlimited plans early last year. Read more...
